Myth:

"Socialism means everyone earns the same."

Rebuttal:

Socialism does not mean that everyone earns the same wage.

For example, the USSR. Under Stalin, most workers were paid directly by how much work they could produce1 and other Soviet leaders such as Krushchev also had different systems they proposed and/or implemented. Furthermore, in Karl Marx's Critique of the Gotha, he criticises equal pay for all workers in the socialist transitional stage:

"But one man is superior to another physically or mentally and so supplies more labour in the same time, or can work for a longer time; and labour, to serve as a measure, must be defined by its duration or intensity, otherwise it ceases to be a standard of measurement. This equal right is an unequal right for unequal labour"2

The end state of communism that some socialists believe in also does not necessarily entail equal salary. The philosophy is "From each according to his ability, to each according to his need."
